
## Market Stalls

* **Grocer — Fatima al-Khadra.** A stout widow in her fifties who runs the vegetable stall
  with iron discipline. She notices everything, remembers every face, and keeps a mental
  ledger of who owes what. Suspicious of strangers by default, but fiercely loyal once she
  decides someone is honest. She is the village's unofficial gossip network. Gifting her
  something useful softens her fast, but she never forgets a theft she witnessed.

* **Seller of household items — Yusuf ibn Tariq.** A wiry, fast-talking man who sells pots,
  rope, candles, and anything else people need. He is cheerful on the surface but calculating
  underneath. He treats every interaction as a negotiation and respects cunning more than
  honesty. The easiest NPC to bribe, but his friendship is shallow, and he gossips about
  everyone, including the player.

* **Rotating out-of-town travelers.** These change week to week. Some are friendly, some are
  hostile, and a few are fellow outsiders who understand the player's isolation. They carry
  unusual items not found in the village, making them valuable trading partners but
  unpredictable allies. They leave before deep trust can form.

* **Livestock dealer — Old Hamid.** A stooped, sun-darkened man who smells like goats and
  speaks in proverbs nobody else understands. He is kind in a gruff, wordless way, often
  offering water to anyone who lingers near his pen. He is nearly deaf, which means he rarely
  witnesses thefts and is one of the safest NPCs to approach early on. He is desperately
  poor and deeply grateful for small gifts.

## Warehouse

* **Warehouse manager — Rafiq al-Amin.** A meticulous, humorless man who counts every sack
  of grain twice. He trusts nobody and keeps the warehouse locked at night. He reports
  suspicious activity to the guards immediately. Useful as a friend late-game because he
  controls the village's food supply, but earning his trust requires patience and proof of
  honest work. He respects consistency above all else.

* **Warehouse hand — Salim.** A young, broad-shouldered laborer who does whatever Rafiq tells
  him. He is simple, good-natured, and easily confused. He sometimes leaves doors unlocked
  by accident. He drinks too much on feast days and talks about wanting to leave the village.
  He is sympathetic to outsiders because he feels trapped himself.

* **Warehouse hand — Nura.** Salim's older sister. Quiet, observant, and far smarter than
  her position suggests. She notices the player before anyone else does and watches without
  reporting. She has her own reasons for keeping secrets. Befriending her unlocks information
  about guard patrol routes and warehouse schedules.

## Blacksmith

* **Blacksmith — Ibrahim al-Hadid.** A massive, scarred man with burned forearms and a
  gentle voice. He was once a soldier and carries a deep weariness from that life. He
  respects actions over words, which makes the player's silence less of an obstacle with
  him. He is slow to trust but unshakeable once committed. He is the gateway to the military
  career path: if the player earns his friendship, he puts in a word with the guard captain.

* **Apprentice — Tarik.** A fourteen-year-old with a quick temper and something to prove. He
  resents being treated like a child and admires anyone who seems dangerous or independent.
  He is reckless, easily impressed by theft, and the first NPC who might actively help the
  player steal, though his help comes with risk because he is loud and clumsy. His loyalty
  is real but immature.

## Mill

* **Miller — Abbas ibn Musa.** A flour-dusted, round-bellied man who laughs too loudly and
  talks to his millstone like it is a companion. He is the most openly warm person in the
  village, but his friendliness is indiscriminate, which means the player is not special to
  him. He gives food scraps to anyone who asks and is chronically in debt. He has a gambling
  problem he hides from the preacher. His poverty makes him a good early shelter candidate.

## Church

* **Preacher — Father Domingo.** A thin, intense Mozarabic Christian who serves the small
  church. He is torn between genuine compassion and rigid morality. He watches the player
  with a mix of pity and suspicion. He is the gateway to the religious career path and will
  offer sanctuary if the player demonstrates penitence through giving rather than taking. He
  is deeply conflicted about the cultural mixing in the village and sometimes preaches
  against it, then privately helps Muslim neighbors. His trust, once earned, comes with
  moral expectations.

## Workshop

* **Artisan — Zahra bint Khalil.** A ceramicist in her thirties who makes tiles, bowls, and
  decorative pieces. She is proud, private, and absorbed in her craft. She barely notices the
  player unless they interfere with her work. She values beauty and skill and can be won over
  by bringing her unusual materials. She is the gateway to the scholar career path: her
  workshop contains books, and she knows the itinerant scholars who pass through. She speaks
  fragments of several languages, which makes her one of the few NPCs who might partially
  understand the player.

## Tailor

* **Tailor — Maryam al-Khayyata.** A precise, soft-spoken woman who makes and mends clothing
  for the entire village. She is observant in a quiet way, noticing when someone's clothes
  are torn or foreign-cut. She is the first NPC to recognize the player as a foreigner rather
  than a criminal. She is sympathetic but cautious, offering small kindnesses (mending a
  sleeve, leaving cloth scraps where the player can find them) without openly associating
  with a stranger. She fears the guards and avoids confrontation.

## Barracks

* **Guard Captain — Capitán Rodrigo.** A weathered career soldier who enforces order without
  cruelty but also without mercy. He views the player as a threat to stability, not a person.
  He cannot be bribed and cannot be befriended through gifts alone. Respect must be earned
  through action. He is the one who recruits the player into the military path if their
  criminal reputation is high enough: he would rather channel a dangerous outsider than let
  them run loose.

* **Guard — Hassan.** A young, nervous guard who joined because his family needed the income.
  He follows orders but hesitates before violence. He is the most likely guard to look the
  other way if the player is non-threatening. He is haunted by a past arrest that went badly
  and does not want blood on his hands again.

* **Guard — Diego.** The opposite of Hassan. He enjoys authority, picks fights with market
  vendors, and views the player as sport. He is the most dangerous patrol to encounter at
  night. He is not corrupt, just cruel. The other guards dislike him but tolerate him because
  he is effective.

## School

* **Teacher — Maestro al-Rashid.** An elderly scholar who runs a small school for the
  village children. He is patient, curious, and the most educated person in the village. He
  is fascinated by languages and might be the first NPC to attempt real communication with
  the player through gestures and drawings. He is physically frail and too poor to offer
  shelter, but his friendship opens doors because everyone in the village respects him. He is
  the other gateway to the scholar path.

## Fields

* **Field manager — Qadir ibn Yusuf.** A practical, sunburned man who runs the agricultural
  operations with quiet competence. He cares about the harvest and little else. He judges
  people by their willingness to work and is one of the few NPCs who might offer the player
  informal labor for food. He is not warm, but he is fair. He has no patience for
  dishonesty and will cut ties permanently if he catches the player stealing from the fields.

* **Wheat farmer — Ines.** A weather-hardened peasant woman who sings while she works and
  stops singing when strangers approach. She is wary but not hostile. She lost a son to
  bandits last year and sees danger everywhere. She is deeply poor and deeply generous to
  anyone she trusts, making her an ideal shelter candidate if the player can break through
  her fear.

* **Beekeeper — Bashir.** A solitary old man who tends the hives at the edge of the fields.
  He prefers bees to people and speaks as little as possible, which means the player's
  silence does not bother him at all. He trades honey for anything he needs and is the most
  self-sufficient person in the village. Hard to befriend because he does not seek company,
  but once bonded, he is the most reliable ally in the game.

* **Shepherd — Young Layla.** A girl of sixteen who watches the sheep on the pasture. She is
  curious, fearless, and the only person in the village who approaches the player first. She
  leaves small gifts (an apple, a piece of cheese) near the alley without being asked. Her
  trust is easy to earn but puts her at risk, because the guards watch her once they notice
  her interest in the stranger.

* **Flax worker — Tomás.** A quiet, broad man who processes flax into linen. He is a
  converted Moor who keeps his head down and avoids attention. He recognizes something
  familiar in the player's outsider status and is silently sympathetic. He communicates
  through small, deliberate gestures. He can teach the player how to work flax for food,
  providing a survival path that does not require theft.

## Manor

* **Local lord — Don Álvaro de Calatrava.** A shrewd, well-educated minor noble who governs
  the manor with a merchant's pragmatism. He tolerates the cultural mixing because it makes
  the village prosperous. He is neither kind nor cruel: he is transactional. The player is
  invisible to him until they become either useful or disruptive. He controls everything and
  everyone answers to him ultimately. He is the final gatekeeper for the merchant career
  path.

* **Steward — Munir.** Don Álvaro's right hand, a former merchant who manages the manor's
  finances and daily operations. He is smooth, political, and always calculating advantage.
  He is the first person at the manor to notice the player and views them as either a tool
  or a problem to be managed. He can be a powerful ally if the player proves useful, but his
  loyalty is to the lord, not to individuals.

* **Maid — Catalina.** A young servant at the manor who slips food to beggars when the
  steward is not looking. She is kind, overworked, and trapped in service. She is terrified
  of being caught helping the player but does it anyway because her faith demands compassion.
  She provides a fragile lifeline to the manor's resources.
